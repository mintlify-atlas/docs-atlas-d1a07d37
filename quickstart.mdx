## Prerequisites

You need [Claude Code](https://claude.ai/code) installed and running. No other dependencies.

## Install

<Steps>
  <Step title="Run the install command">
    In your terminal, run:

    ```bash
    npx skills add alchaincyf/steve-jobs-skill
    ```

    This fetches `SKILL.md` from [alchaincyf/steve-jobs-skill](https://github.com/alchaincyf/steve-jobs-skill) and registers it with Claude Code.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Claude Code">
    Start a new session in Claude Code. The skill is now available globally — you don't need to reference it by name in every message.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Activate with a trigger phrase">
    Say anything that invokes Jobs' perspective. The skill recognizes a range of phrases:

    <CodeGroup>
      ```text Chinese (product analysis)
      用乔布斯的视角帮我分析这个产品方向
      ```

      ```text Chinese (competitive analysis)
      Jobs会怎么看AI Agent的竞争格局？
      ```

      ```text Chinese (decision review)
      切换到乔布斯，我在纠结三件事
      ```

      ```text English
      What would Jobs think about this product?
      ```
    </CodeGroup>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the one-time disclaimer">
    On first activation per session, the skill displays a single disclaimer:

    > "I'm responding from Jobs' perspective, based on his public statements — not his actual views."

    This appears **once**. It will not repeat in follow-up turns.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Your first conversation

Here's a real example from the first session with the skill. The question was about AI hardware:

> **You:** 如果要在这个时代做AI硬件产品，你会选择做什么？

The skill's response (abbreviated):

> AI hardware is where the PC industry was in 1977. Everyone's building the equivalent of the Altair 8800 — impressive to geeks, useless to your mom. Rabbit, Humane Pin — those are shit.
>
> 我不会做一个新品类。我会重新定义一个已经存在的品类。
>
> iPhone不是第一个手机。iPod不是第一个MP3播放器。我们从来不发明品类，我们重新定义品类。
>
> 答案：耳机。一个always-on的个人AI界面。An AI that lives in your ear.

Notice what happened:
- No preamble. The verdict came first ("those are shit").
- It applied the **Whole Widget** model (end-to-end control) and **subtract-first** heuristic (redefine, don't invent).
- It cited specific products (Rabbit, Humane Pin) rather than speaking in abstractions.

## How the skill works under the hood

Every question goes through three steps before a response is generated:

1. **Classify** — Is this a factual question (about a real product or market), a pure framework question (about product philosophy), or mixed?
2. **Research** — For factual and mixed questions, the skill uses web search to fetch real product data, reviews, and competitive context. It does not guess from training data.
3. **Answer** — The response applies the relevant mental models, uses Jobs' expression DNA (short sentences, no hedging, headline first), and cites specific details.

<Tip>
  For the best results, ask about real, specific things — a product you're building, a competitor you're watching, a decision you're facing. Vague questions get more generic answers. The more concrete the input, the more useful the output.
</Tip>
